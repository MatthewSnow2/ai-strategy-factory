## Terminology

*   **Generative AI (gen AI):** A type of artificial intelligence that can create new content, such as text, images, or code, based on patterns learned from training data.
*   **Data Poisoning:** A cybersecurity attack where malicious data is introduced into a machine learning model's training dataset, causing the model to learn incorrect or biased information, leading to manipulated outputs.
*   **Hallucinations:** In the context of AI (especially generative AI), when the model generates outputs that are plausible but factually incorrect, nonsensical, or not supported by its training data.
*   **Large Language Model (LLM):** A type of generative AI model, typically with billions of parameters, trained on vast amounts of text data to understand, summarize, generate, and predict human-like language.
*   **Hybrid Cloud:** A cloud computing environment that combines a public cloud, a private cloud, and/or on-premises infrastructure, allowing data and applications to be shared between them.
*   **Container Platform:** A software platform that packages applications and their dependencies into standardized units (containers) for consistent deployment across different computing environments, often enhancing energy efficiency and resource utilization.

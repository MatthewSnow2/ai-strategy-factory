## Key Concepts & Frameworks

*   **The Agentic Organization:** A new organizational paradigm where humans, virtual **AI agents**, and physical AI agents work side by side at scale, driving value creation at near-zero marginal cost. This represents the largest organizational shift since the industrial and digital revolutions.
*   **Five Pillars of the Agentic Organization:** These are the core enterprise elements that will be transformed in the AI era. They include:
    *   **Business Model:** Shifts to AI-native channels for hyperpersonalization, AI-first workflows, and proprietary data as a key differentiator.
    *   **Operating Model:** Evolves to flat networks of empowered, outcome-aligned **agentic teams**, reimagining work as AI-first with humans primarily "above the loop."
    *   **Governance:** Becomes real-time, data-driven, and embedded within workflows, with humans maintaining final accountability and oversight.
    *   **Workforce, People, and Culture:** Adapts to a hybrid workforce with new talent profiles (M-shaped, T-shaped, AI-augmented) and a culture focused on continuous learning, trust, and ethical guidance.

## Terminology

*   **Agentic Organization:** A new paradigm where humans and AI agents (virtual and physical) collaborate at scale to create value.
*   **AI Agents:** Intelligent virtual or physical entities designed to perform tasks, augment human capabilities, or automate workflows.
*   **Agentic Team:** A small group of multidisciplinary humans who own and supervise underlying AI workflows to deliver clear end-to-end business outcomes.
*   **Agent Factory:** A concept referring to a large collection of specialized AI agents working together on an end-to-end process, supervised by a smaller human team.
*   **AI-first Workflow:** A redesigned end-to-end business process where AI agents are central, with humans positioned "above the loop" for strategic oversight or selectively "within the loop" where human contact is essential.
*   **M-shaped Supervisors:** New talent profiles representing broad generalists fluent in AI, orchestrating agents and the hybrid workforce across various domains.
*   **T-shaped Experts:** New talent profiles referring to deep specialists who reimagine workflows, handle exceptions, and safeguard the quality of agentic systems, with extensive subject matter expertise.
*   **AI-augmented Frontline Workers:** Employees in roles like sales or customer service who leverage AI to reduce time on systems and focus more on interpersonal tasks requiring human touch.
*   **Agentic AI Mesh:** An architectural approach providing reusable, high-performing "atomic" agents and data products, equipped with technical safety guardrails, to enable controlled democratization and innovation of IT systems and data.
*   **Agent-to-Agent Protocols:** New communication methods that allow AI agents, IT systems, and devices to interact and integrate more easily and cheaply than traditional middleware or APIs.
*   **Dynamic Sourcing:** A flexible strategy for acquiring AI technologies and solutions that adapts quickly to rapidly evolving AI products and avoids vendor lock-in, while protecting proprietary organizational context.
*   **Lighthouse Domains:** Strategic areas or business functions within an organization that have the potential for substantial value creation with AI, are visible, and possess sufficient technological maturity for early implementation and learning.
*   **Two-pizza team:** A concept referring to a team size small enough to be fed by two pizzas, often cited as an ideal size for agile human teams (mentioned as a limitation in the agentic era).
*   **Dunbar’s number:** A cognitive limit to the number of people one can maintain stable social relationships with, also mentioned as a constraint for human team sizes.
